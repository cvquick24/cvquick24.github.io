Data/Question/Purpose:

We are using a data set that we obtained from data world titled “Game of Thrones Deaths” that tracks character deaths in the TV show, “Game of Thrones.” We are seeking to find what trends exist in the deaths throughout the Game of Thrones universe. This is one of our favorite shows and we were excited to take an in-depth look at the show’s data.

Steps taken in analysis and visualization:

The dataset we began our project with was very helpful in planning what sort of visualizations we could create, however it was missing many key elements for what we wanted to achieve and almost every visualization required between 2-6 hours each of data cleaning and organization first. The way the original dataset was organized meant that we had to manually filter it by unique and non-unique characters for determining who died or killed the most often. We also had to create new tables that kept track of things like the number of deaths per episode and the number of times certain characters used each kind of weapon. Not to mention, we had to manually geocode the data, meaning we assigned coordinate values to every location in Westeros where a death occurred. These coordinates were calculated by placing points on a map of Westeros and using Interworks CBI Studios software to generate longitude and latitude. The map we decided to use as our background layer is a fan-made map of westeros that seemed just detailed enough to provide spatial context without being distracting. We then put all of the data into Tableau where we were able to create 5 visualizations. Firstly, we included a very simple bar graph showcasing the amount of deaths per character. This visualization displayed the number of deaths as “2” for “Beric Dondarrion” and “1” for “Everyone else.” This graph is very simplistic and we mostly decided to include as a joke about how this specific character is killed during the show, but later revived and killed off again. We utilized 2 separate stacked bar charts to display the kill counts of the show’s characters with the highest number of kills and which weapons they most often employed. One of these charts includes Daenerys Targaryen, Cersei Lannister, and Arya Stark, since they hold the record for the 3 highest kill counts, so far greater than the 10 (male) characters after them in the ranking, that including any of them in the same chart made the men’s kills too small to make out. The other chart contains Jon Snow, Grey Worm, The Hound, Bronn, Daario Naharis, Tormund, Ramsay Bolton, and Theon Greyjoy, the 4th through 13th top-ranking killers. Another chart we made is a line graph tracking on-screen deaths per episode over the course of the series. We included reference lines to mark the beginning of each new season and also denoted change in season through gradient shades of gray, marking important battles or events where the death toll spikes. We also made a tree map displaying the proportion of kills committed by each house, ordered from greatest to least. Finally, we made a map of every death in Westeros, displaying which areas of the world the most characters have died in.
